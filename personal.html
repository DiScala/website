About My PhD

My PhD is about: Knowledge Enhanced AI Advice Systems for Trustworthy High-Stakes Decision Support

My PhD Goal: to improve the trustworthiness of AI advice, by introducing knowledge-enhanced self-reflection to advice providing AI systems. This leads to users better being able to understand, follow/trace and evaluate the usefulness of the AI answers, so they can appropriately trust AI for decision support (leading to safer, more productive, more objective, more accountable, decisions).
Generative AI systems like ChatGPT are increasingly being used for help with decision making. However, for assistance with making tough decisions that lead to high-risk consequences (e.g., medical, judicial, business advice), these systems and the knowledge they provide have to be sufficiently trustworthy. In this PhD project, I aim to identify, formalize and capture different aspects that lead to appropriate trust (not under- or overtrust) in AI advice, to design hybrid AI systems that are a composite of data driven LLMs and knowledge driven models (Knowledge Graphs, Ontologies) that are able to adhere to these trust aspects, and to evaluate the trustworthiness of state-of-the-art AI advice, including existing software and proposed new methods, through both objective and user-centric evaluations.
